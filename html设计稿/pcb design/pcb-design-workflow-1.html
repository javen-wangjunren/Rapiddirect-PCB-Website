Idea & Definition

Step 1

Project Requirement & Alignment

Deeply align with customers on project goals, application scenarios, performance metrics, cost, and delivery requirements. Output the "Project Requirement Specification" to clarify key constraints (e.g., high-speed signals, EMC, layer stackup) ensuring the design is 100% matched to your needs.

PCB Design

Step 2

Schematic Design & Review

Complete the circuit schematic design based on requirements, including power supply, signal chain, and control logic. Conduct concurrent DRC and cross-team reviews to eliminate functional defects and logic loopholes early on.

Step 3

PCB Layout & Component Placement

Perform PCB layout and routing based on the schematic. Optimize component placement, signal routing, and power delivery networks (PDN). Focus on high-speed signals, impedance control, differential pairs, and EMC routing while balancing performance, cost, and manufacturability.

Validation & Simulation

Step 4

Engineering Simulation & Verification

Conduct SI (Signal Integrity), PI (Power Integrity), EMC/EMI, and thermal analysis for critical signal paths. Proactively identify and resolve crosstalk, power noise, electromagnetic radiation, and thermal risks to prevent post-production rework.

Step 5

DFM/DFA Review & Optimization

Execute comprehensive DFM (Design for Manufacturing) and DFA (Design for Assembly) reviews. Verify trace width/spacing, vias, pads, and solder mask to optimize Gerber files and BOM, ensuring seamless transition to PCB fabrication and SMT assembly.

Gerber Output

Step 6

Design File Finalization & Handover

Output a complete manufacturing file package, including Gerber/drill files, BOM, pick-and-place files, assembly drawings, and test instructions. Files undergo multiple verifications to ensure full compatibility with production requirements.

Manufacturing

We provide one-stop services from PCB prototyping and SMT assembly to mass production. Once the design is finalized, it seamlessly transitions into manufacturing, significantly shortening your project timeline.
